Candlestick vs. Line — key tradeoffs

What candlesticks add:
· Volatility at a glance — tall wick = turbulent period
· Beat/miss is clearer than drift shading — color directly communicates period outcome
· Big events (Boosts, Hits) read as standout candles, not subtle curve deviations
· Stock-literate users (your target demo) read these instantly

What candlesticks cost:
· Pulse precision is lost — you can no longer drag to any moment; the cursor snaps to period closes. "What did my margin look like on March 15th?" becomes "What did the week of Mar 10 close at?"
· Candle width scales with the data window — 1m shows daily candles, 3y shows monthly candles; the visual language stays consistent but density changes
· The step-up event (loan resolves) now spans a candle rather than being a crisp inflection point

Hybrid option: Line chart as the primary Horizon with candlestick as an optional view toggle in the graph header. Pulse stays point-in-time on the line; switch to candles to audit a specific period's volatility.
